## 6 â€” Lievrouw and Finn's Model

A 3D matrix (1990) designed to **bridge interpersonal and mass communication**.

> [!abstract] The Three Dimensions
> 1. **Control** â€” balance of power between sender and receiver
> 2. **Involvement** â€” physical or psychological distance (high vs. low)
> 3. **Temporality** â€” simultaneous (live) vs. nonsimultaneous interaction

> [!info] Core Argument
> All communication is **mediated** â€” either by technology or the human senses.

**Spectrum:** Maps systems from Face-to-Face Interaction (1) to Database Systems (14).

## 7 â€” Foulger's Ecological Model

A reflexive model (2004) where **roles shift based on the media environment**.

> [!abstract] Fluid Roles
> - Participants are both **Creators** and **Consumers**
> - Roles are **reflexive**: people learn to create by consuming others' messages

> [!info] Environmental Rules
> 1. Messages are created/consumed using **Language**
> 2. Language occurs within the context of **Media**
> 3. People invent and evolve the media they use

> [!warning] Reality Check
> - **Subjectivity** â€” interpretation is always imperfect due to language/media limits
> - **Selective Attention** â€” consumers choose from a multiplicity of messages
